Strength Assessment

Home · ~30 min · Light only ✓ Saved

Why we're doing this

This is an assessment, not a workout. We're checking movement quality, finding any weak links, and seeing what loads make sense. Stop short of failure on every exercise.

You already golfed today. Tonight is light. Real strength work starts Wednesday.

Tap "How to" on any exercise for step-by-step instructions.

Equipment available: 2× 15-lb dumbbells, 2× 5-lb dumbbells, 2× 3-lb dumbbells, band gym (platform + bar), ab wheel, 6-lb medicine ball.
Block 1 · Warmup
Movement Prep
5 minutes · No equipment
Arm circles + shoulder rolls
10 forward, 10 back, each direction
How:
  1. Stand tall, arms straight out to your sides at shoulder height (like a T).
  2. Make slow controlled circles forward — small at first, gradually larger. 10 reps.
  3. Reverse direction. 10 reps backward.
  4. Drop arms to sides. Roll shoulders backward 10 times, then forward 10 times.
Why: Wakes up the shoulder joints and rotator cuff. Cheap insurance against injury.
Trunk rotations (standing)
15 each side, slow & controlled
How:
  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Hold a club, broomstick, or just your arms across your chest at shoulder height.
  3. Keep your hips facing forward — rotate your upper body to the right as far as comfortable.
  4. Return to center. Rotate to the left.
  5. That's 1 rep each side. Do 15 each direction, slow and controlled.
Key: Rotation comes from the upper back (thoracic spine), not the lower back. Keep your belt buckle pointing forward the whole time.
Bodyweight squats
10 reps, controlled tempo
How:
  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  2. Arms out in front for balance (or hands clasped at chest).
  3. Sit back like you're sitting into a chair behind you. Knees stay tracking over your toes.
  4. Lower until your thighs are at least parallel to the floor (or as low as comfortable).
  5. Drive through your heels to stand up. Squeeze glutes at the top.
  6. 2 seconds down, 1 second up. 10 reps.
Form check: Knees shouldn't cave inward. Chest stays up. Heels stay on the ground.
World's greatest stretch
5 reps each side
How (this is one continuous movement):
  1. Start in a high plank position (top of a pushup).
  2. Step your right foot forward to the OUTSIDE of your right hand. You're now in a deep lunge.
  3. Place your right hand on the ground inside your right foot. Reach your left hand straight up toward the ceiling, rotating your chest open to the left.
  4. Hold for 1 second. Return left hand to the ground.
  5. Step back to plank. That's 1 rep on the right.
  6. Repeat with left foot forward. Alternate 5 each side.
Why this matters for golf: Opens up hips, t-spine, shoulders, and hamstrings — all the things that limit a full backswing and follow-through.
Easier option: Just do 5 forward lunges each leg if the rotation is too much tonight.
Block 2 · Movement Screen
Find the Weak Links
10 minutes
Single-leg balance (eyes open)
30 sec each leg
How:
  1. Stand barefoot or in socks on a flat floor.
  2. Lift one foot off the ground — knee bent, foot held about 6 inches up.
  3. Look at a fixed point on the wall in front of you.
  4. Time how long you can hold steady without putting the foot down OR catching yourself with the wall/furniture.
  5. If you make 30 seconds clean, you're good — stop there. Otherwise, record what you got.
  6. Switch legs.
Why golfers care: The downswing requires shifting your weight onto your lead leg and rotating around it. Poor single-leg balance = unstable contact and inconsistent strikes.
Overhead reach test (wall test)
Hold 15 sec
How:
  1. Stand with your back against a wall. Heels about 6 inches out from the wall.
  2. Press your lower back flat against the wall (no gap behind it). Keep it flat the whole test.
  3. Raise both arms straight up overhead, palms facing each other.
  4. Try to touch the backs of your hands to the wall above your head WITHOUT letting your lower back come off the wall.
  5. Hold 15 seconds. Note any restriction.
What it tells us: If your lower back arches off the wall when arms go up, you have tight lats or shoulders compensating. Common in tall golfers. Fixable, but we need to know it's there.
Plank hold
Max time — cap at 90 sec
How:
  1. Get on the floor in a forearm plank position: forearms flat, elbows directly under shoulders, toes on the ground, body in a straight line from head to heels.
  2. Squeeze your glutes and brace your core (like someone's about to punch your stomach).
  3. Hold. Time it.
  4. Stop when form breaks — hips sag down OR pike up toward the ceiling. Don't grind through bad form.
  5. Cap at 90 seconds even if you can go longer. We're assessing, not testing limits.
What it tells us: Plank time reflects core endurance, which stabilizes the spine during the swing. A weak core leaks power and causes back pain.
Pushups (max in one set)
Stop at form failure or 25
How:
  1. Start in a high plank: hands directly under shoulders, body in a straight line, core braced.
  2. Lower yourself by bending elbows — they should track at about 45° from your body, not flared out wide.
  3. Lower until your chest is about a fist's width from the floor.
  4. Press back up. That's 1 rep.
  5. Count reps. Stop when form breaks (hips sag, chest doesn't get low enough) OR when you hit 25.
Easier option (record this): Drop to knees instead of toes. Same motion, easier load. Just note "knee" in the type field.
What it tells us: Upper body pressing strength. We don't need bodybuilder numbers — we need enough to support a stable upper body through the swing.
Block 3 · Rotational
Golf-Specific Movements
10 minutes · Light intensity
Med ball chest pass to wall
2 sets × 8 reps, 6-lb ball
How:
  1. Find an open wall (concrete, brick, or garage door work; avoid drywall — it'll dent).
  2. Stand 3-4 feet from the wall, feet shoulder-width apart.
  3. Hold the 6-lb medicine ball with both hands at your chest, elbows out.
  4. Throw the ball HARD straight into the wall (chest-pass style, like a basketball).
  5. Catch the ball on the rebound at your chest.
  6. That's 1 rep. Do 8 reps. Rest 60 seconds. Do another set of 8.
Tonight only: ~70% effort. We're priming the pattern, not maxing out. Save the real power for later in the week.
Med ball rotational throw
2 sets × 6 each side, 70% effort
This is the most golf-specific exercise we have:
  1. Stand sideways to a sturdy wall (right shoulder pointing at the wall to start).
  2. Position yourself about 3-4 feet from the wall.
  3. Feet shoulder-width apart, knees soft.
  4. Hold the 6-lb medicine ball with both hands at your right hip.
  5. Quickly rotate your torso toward the wall (like swinging a baseball bat) and throw the ball HARD into the wall.
  6. Catch the ball on rebound. Reset to starting position at the right hip.
  7. Do 6 reps on the right side. Then turn around (left shoulder to wall) and do 6 on the left.
  8. Rest 60 seconds. Do one more set each side.
Key form points: Drive with your hips first, then the torso, then the arms — same sequence as the golf swing. Keep your eyes on the wall, not the ball.
Tonight only: 70% effort. We're learning the movement, not chasing power yet.
Goblet squat
2 sets × 8 reps, 15-lb dumbbell
How:
  1. Hold ONE 15-lb dumbbell vertically against your chest with both hands cupping the top end (so it's "hanging" in front of you like a goblet).
  2. Keep elbows pointing down, dumbbell tight to your chest.
  3. Feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  4. Squat down — sit back into your hips, keep chest up, knees track over toes.
  5. Go as deep as you can with good form (ideally thighs parallel or lower).
  6. Drive through heels to stand up. That's 1 rep.
  7. Do 8 reps. Rest 90 seconds. Do another set of 8.
Tonight is a feel-out set. If 15-lb feels heavy with bad form, drop to a 5-lb and note it. If it's easy, that tells us you need more weight in Week 1.
Band gym — light rotational pull
1 set × 10 each side, lightest band
Setting up the band gym (LalaHigh):
  1. Set the platform down on the floor.
  2. Attach the LIGHTEST band/resistance level only — tonight is about learning the device, not loading it up.
  3. Stand on the platform with both feet to anchor it.
  4. Hold the bar with both hands at hip height, parallel to the floor.
The exercise:
  1. Stand sideways on the platform (right hip facing forward).
  2. Hold the bar with both hands at your right hip.
  3. Rotate your torso to the LEFT — pulling the bar across your body to your left side, like swinging a club through impact.
  4. Slow return back to the right hip. That's 1 rep.
  5. 10 reps. Then turn around and do 10 from the left side rotating right.
If anything feels weird or unstable about the band setup, stop and note it. Better to figure out the equipment now than mid-workout later this week.
Block 4 · Cooldown
Reset for Tomorrow
5 minutes
Hip flexor stretch
30 sec each side
How:
  1. Kneel on your right knee with your left foot flat on the ground in front of you (90° lunge position).
  2. Cushion the right knee with a folded towel if it's uncomfortable.
  3. Squeeze your right glute, tuck your pelvis under (like pulling your belt buckle up toward your ribs).
  4. Gently push your hips forward until you feel a stretch in the front of the right hip/thigh.
  5. Hold 30 seconds. Switch legs.
Why golfers need this: Tight hip flexors limit hip rotation and force your lower back to do the work. Common in anyone who sits a lot.
Doorway pec stretch
30 sec each side
How:
  1. Find a doorway. Stand in the opening.
  2. Bend your right elbow at 90° and place your forearm flat against the door frame, elbow at shoulder height.
  3. Step forward with your right foot through the doorway, keeping the forearm pressed against the frame.
  4. You should feel a stretch across the front of your right shoulder and chest.
  5. Hold 30 seconds. Switch sides.
Why: Tight pecs round the shoulders forward, killing your turn. This opens them up.
Cat-cow + child's pose
2 minutes total
Cat-cow (8-10 reps, slow):
  1. Get on your hands and knees. Hands under shoulders, knees under hips.
  2. Cow: Drop your belly toward the floor, lift your chest and chin up, arch your back. Inhale.
  3. Cat: Reverse — round your back toward the ceiling, tuck your chin to chest. Exhale.
  4. Slow and smooth, 8-10 reps total.
Then child's pose (60 seconds):
  1. From hands-and-knees, sit back onto your heels.
  2. Reach your arms forward on the floor. Forehead down on the floor or a pillow.
  3. Breathe slow. Hold for ~60 seconds.
Why: Decompresses your spine after a day on the course. Calms the nervous system. Good last move before bed.
Water + protein within 30 min
16-20 oz water, light protein snack
Light protein options:
  • Glass of milk or chocolate milk
  • Greek yogurt
  • Protein shake (one scoop with water or milk)
  • Handful of nuts + a piece of fruit

Why: You sweated a lot today. Rehydrating + a little protein in the next 30 minutes is the cheapest recovery move there is.
