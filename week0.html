Morning Session
Alternate course chipping/putting · 90 min · Before 12pm
5 min. You know the drill now.
⏱ ~30 min · The big one. Yesterday 50yd was 0/10 inside 10ft.
The 30/40 ladder (with 40 as the max available distance):
  1. Hit 10 balls at 30 yards.
  2. Hit 10 balls at 35 yards.
  3. Hit 10 balls at 40 yards.
What we're solving:
  • Yesterday every iron missed LEFT. For a lefty pushing left = open clubface.
  • Try gripping the club slightly stronger (more knuckles on top hand). See if it brings the face square.
  • Focus on starting the ball at the right edge of your target line.
Count results: How many of 30 finished within 15 ft? Within 10? Record via Share button.
⏱ ~20 min · 3-putt killer drill
The drill:
  1. Place tees at 20, 30, 40, 50 feet from a hole.
  2. Put your putter head flat 3 feet PAST the hole — that's the "danger zone."
  3. Hit 3 putts from each distance. Goal: stop the ball between the hole and putter (3-foot window past).
  4. Short is worse than long — we never want to leave a lag short.
Mental cue: "Past the hole" — every lag's job is to reach the hole, not die to it.
⏱ ~20 min · 15 chips, mixed lies
  1. 5 chips from clean fairway lie
  2. 5 chips from deeper grass / rough
  3. 5 chips from a "tweener" lie
Goal: Land each in 6-foot circle. Score yourself /15.
Storms incoming. Hydrate, eat, rest.

Morning Block — 45 min
50 putts to a quarter or small target from 8ft.
Build muscle memory for overlap grip.
  1. Any iron. Set up in front of a mirror.
  2. Practice taking your grip from scratch — bottom hand first, then top hand overlapping.
  3. Check mirror: knuckles visible on top hand, V's pointing toward trail shoulder.
  4. Slow half-speed swings, checking grip stays neutral through impact.
  5. 15 minutes of reps.
Most valuable thing today. New grip needs 1000+ reps before it feels automatic.
⏱ ~20 min · Define your routine, practice 20 reps in living room.
A good routine has 5-7 steps. Example:
  1. Stand behind ball, pick exact target
  2. Visualize the shot shape
  3. Take 1-2 practice swings, feeling the shot
  4. Walk into the ball, clubface to target first
  5. Set your feet
  6. One look at target, one look at ball
  7. Pull the trigger — no second-guessing
Then text Dad your routine — we use it as your tournament routine.

Pre-round Warmup — 45 min
PW → 9i → 7i → 5i → driver. 5-8 balls each.
The Round — 18 Holes (Tracked)
Real-time, not at the end.
Driver vs. 3-wood — which hits more fairways?
Today's decision: Driver hit 3/14 fairways Monday. Not tournament-ready.
  • Every par 4 / par 5: ask "driver or 3-wood?"
  • Try 3-wood on a couple holes where you'd normally hit driver.
  • Track which club gives you a higher % of fairways.
  • Goal: find the club that gives 60%+ fairways. That's your tournament tee club.
Coach's truth: 3-wood in fairway beats driver in trees every time.
