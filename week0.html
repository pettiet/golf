Morning Session
Alternate course chipping/putting area · 90 min · Before 12pm
5 min. You know the drill now.
⏱ ~30 min · The big one. Yesterday was 0/10 inside 10 ft.
The ladder drill:
  1. Pace off 40 yards. Hit 10 balls to a target. Note where the ball lands relative to the target (short/long/left/right).
  2. Move to 50 yards. Hit 10 balls.
  3. Move to 60 yards. Hit 10 balls.
What we're solving:
  • Most amateurs miss 50-yarders short. Yesterday everything was LEFT. So today: focus on starting the ball at the right-half of the target.
  • For a LEFTY pushing left = open clubface at impact. Try gripping the club slightly stronger (more knuckles visible on top hand) and see if that brings the face square.
  • Don't try to fix mechanics — just get the ball starting at your line.
Count results: How many of 30 finished within 15 feet? Within 10? Record in share button.
⏱ ~20 min · 3-putt killer drill
The drill:
  1. Place tees at 20, 30, 40, 50 feet from a hole.
  2. Put your putter head flat on the ground, 3 feet PAST the hole — this is the "danger zone."
  3. Hit 3 putts from each distance. Goal: stop the ball BETWEEN the hole and the putter (3-foot window past the hole).
  4. If you leave it short, that's worse than going long. We never want to leave a putt short on a lag.
Mental cue: "Past the hole" — every lag putt's job is to reach the hole, not die to it.
⏱ ~20 min · 15 chips, mix of lies
  1. 5 chips from clean fairway lie (just off the green)
  2. 5 chips from deeper grass / rough
  3. 5 chips from a "tweener" lie (downhill, sidehill, etc.)
Goal: Land each one in a 6-foot circle around the hole. Score yourself out of 15.
If chipping was rated your "worst today" yesterday, this is the work that fixes it.
Hydrate, eat, rest in the afternoon.
⚠ If storms come early: Get the lag putting and 50-yd wedges done first (in that order). Skip chipping if time runs out — we can hit chips at home on the carpet later.
Evening Session
Home · 30 min · Light strength
Dynamic stretches + trunk rotations (5 min)
Two 15-lb dumbbells held at chest (30 lb total), since 15 felt easy.
Hold BOTH 15-lb dumbbells vertically against your chest, side by side. Same goblet position as Monday — chest up, elbows down, sit back into the squat. 3 sets of 8, rest 90 sec between sets.
If 30 lb total feels too heavy with one hand on each, go back to one DB and we'll progress differently.
85% effort. Focus on the weaker (right) side.
Same as Monday's setup, but harder effort and focus on the weaker right side. Do all 3 sets on the right first, then 3 sets on the left. Rest 60 sec between sets.
For a lefty golfer, the right side is the trail side — getting it stronger helps drive the downswing.
15-lb DB, support hand on a chair/bench
  1. Put one knee and one hand on a sturdy chair or low bench.
  2. Other foot on the floor, holding the 15-lb DB in the free hand.
  3. Pull the DB up to your hip, elbow tight to your side. Squeeze the back muscle.
  4. Slow lower. 10 reps. Switch sides.
  5. 3 sets of 10 each side.
Builds the back muscles that stabilize the upper body through impact.
Core finisher. Don't go to failure.
5 min — hamstrings were tight yesterday
Three hamstring stretches, 30 sec each, each leg:
  1. Standing toe touch — feet hip-width, slow fold forward, hang from the hips. Let head drop.
  2. Seated single-leg reach — sit on the floor, one leg straight, other bent in. Reach for toes on the straight leg.
  3. Lying hamstring stretch — lie on back, lift one leg straight up, gently pull toward chest.
Then hip flexor stretch + doorway pec stretch + child's pose (like Monday).

Morning Block
Home · 45 min
Get the new grip feeling on the putter
  1. Find a flat hallway or carpet stretch.
  2. Put a quarter or small target down 8 feet away.
  3. Hit putts at the quarter. Goal: ball stops within an inch of it.
  4. 50 putts total. Focus on starting line + speed.
If you have a putting mirror or alignment aid, use it. Otherwise just track how many of 50 stop within a putter-head's width of the quarter.
Build muscle memory for the overlap grip
  1. Take any iron. Set up in front of a mirror.
  2. Practice taking your grip from scratch — bottom hand first, then top hand overlapping properly.
  3. Check in the mirror: knuckles visible on top hand, V's pointing toward your trail shoulder.
  4. Make slow, half-speed swings checking that the grip stays neutral through impact.
  5. Do this for 15 minutes. Reps build the new pattern.
This is the most valuable thing you can do today. A new grip needs 1000+ reps before it feels automatic. Every rep counts.
⏱ ~20 min · Define your routine
Write down your pre-shot routine, step by step. Then practice it 20 times in your living room with any club. A good routine has 5-7 steps. Example:
  1. Stand behind ball, pick exact target
  2. Visualize the shot shape
  3. Take 1-2 practice swings, feeling the shot
  4. Walk into the ball, set the clubface to target first
  5. Set your feet
  6. One look at target, one look at ball
  7. Pull the trigger — no second-guessing
Then text Dad the routine you wrote — we'll use it as your tournament routine on June 2.
Afternoon Block
Home · 40 min
Same as Tuesday evening's session
Repeat Tuesday's strength: goblet squats 3×8, med ball rotational 3×8 each side, DB row 3×10 each side, plank 2×45 + side plank 2×20.

⏱ ~15 min · Strategic prep
  1. Pull up the course's scorecard / yardage book online if available.
  2. For each hole: identify the safe play off the tee (not always driver).
  3. Note any hazards on holes 1, 18, and the par 3s.
  4. Write down 3 holes you want to play conservatively.
Knowing the course in advance reduces decision fatigue under tournament pressure.
If a weather window opens: Drop the home strength and get to the course for as long as you can. Priorities: lag putting (10 min) → 50-yd wedges (20 min) → done. Don't push it.

Mental rehearsal of Tuesday's round
  1. Find a quiet spot. Close your eyes.
  2. Imagine arriving at the tournament course Tuesday morning. The walk to the first tee.

The Round — 18 Holes
Tracked · Use Round Tracker in the Hub
Fill in real-time at the green. Don't trust memory at the end.
Tournament strategy decision
The decision today: Driver hit 3/14 fairways Monday. That's not a tournament-ready number.
  • On every par 4 and par 5, ask yourself: "Driver or 3-wood?"
  • Try hitting 3-wood off the tee on a couple holes where driver would normally come out.
  • Note: which club gives you a higher % of fairways hit?
  • Find the club that gives you 60%+ fairways at your stock distance. That's your tournament tee club.
Coach's truth: A 3-wood in the fairway beats a driver in the woods every time.
